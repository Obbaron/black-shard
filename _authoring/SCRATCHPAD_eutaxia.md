## 0. THE TWO ORDERS *(the central doctrine)*

### The doctrine [CANON]

**There are two orders.**

- **The Cosmic Order.** The domain of **Sounia**, styled **Dharmachakra** ("wheel of law"; ancient, pre-Yavanna). It is the order that *is*: fixed, serene, obeyed without legislation. **It is incapable of disorder.**
- **The Civic Order.** The domain of **Theureus**. It is the order that must be *kept*. It erodes without vigilance.

**The second must be made to resemble the first.** The Emperor does not invent the civic order. He **upholds** it, and the labour is ours.

### Why the sky is incapable of disorder [CANON]

**Because the disorderly one was cast out of it.**

Once the heavens held eight. **Mania defied Sounia**, who is the order of the sky, and demanded to run as fast as her wanting. **Barjas**, the Arbiter, the dispenser of judgment, **hurled the thunderbolt and struck her out of heaven.**

She was **cast out of divinity** and fell into the lower order.

And she was granted exactly what she asked for. **She runs.** Free at last, as fast as she ever wished, and she has never once escaped and never once will.

**What runs up there is not a member of the heavens. It is a convict, serving a sentence, in public view.**

### Her sentence: the seven [CANON]

Her cycle is **fourteen days: seven out, and seven back.** A pendulum, not an orbit. She is a body that **reverses**, which no proper heavenly body does.

**She counts the week.** Seven out, seven home. **She is the timekeeper of the mortal week: the very thing she was cast out of.** She keeps it for the men who expelled her, she cannot stop, and every fortnight she counts out the seven gods who remain and is reminded that she is not among them.

**The god who would not hold a station is now the clock of every man who does.**

### THE PROOF [CANON, computed]

This is the empirical spine of the whole faith.

| | |
|---|---|
| **378 ÷ 7 = 54 exactly** | The old year was, mathematically, **a seven-day year**. |
| **378 ÷ 8 = 47.25** | The eight-day week **never fit the old year.** It was always ragged. |

**The ancients kept an eight-day week inside a year that was mathematically seven**, while Mania ran seven-and-seven over their heads every fortnight, counting it out for them.

**The sky was saying seven. They counted eight.**

The eighth day was never in the heavens. **No body ever measured it.** It was a human invention, a courtesy extended to a convict, and the year itself refused it for the whole of history.

> **And her punishment is the instrument that convicts her.** She was cast out of the week, and her sentence is to count it. **She testifies against herself, every fortnight, forever.**

*(Also: **378 ÷ 14 = 27**. She completes exactly twenty-seven runs in the old year, one for each day of Monē's course. The sisters were always in conversation.)*

### THE RECKONERS' ANSWER [CANON, computed]

And it is devastating, and Eutaxia has no reply.

| | |
|---|---|
| **378 ÷ 7 = 54** | The old year held **fifty-four perfect weeks.** |
| **361 ÷ 7 = 51 r 4** | His year holds **fifty-one, and leaks four days.** |

> *He gave us the true week, and then destroyed the only year that could contain it. Ours closed. His does not. **Ask him where the four days went.***

**Both sides are correct. Neither can answer the other.** This is the schism, and it fell out of the arithmetic rather than out of anyone's theology.

### What this makes Menneus [CANON]

**An upholder, not an author.** He is **not** the centre of the religion.

**Barjas** passed the judgment. **The gods** expelled her from heaven. But mortals went on giving her a day for centuries, out of habit and sentiment and error, and **the earth lagged behind the sky.**

**Menneus did not innovate.** He brought the calendar into conformity with a ruling that had already been handed down in heaven. **He made the earth resemble the heavens.** It is the most orthodox act in the history of the empire, and it is exactly what the Two Orders doctrine requires of an Emperor.

The Severance is not the founding of a new order. **It is a correction of the civic order to match the cosmic one.** Eight hundred years late.

### The moral, and how to phrase it [CANON]

The lesson of Mania is **not** "you will break the world."

**You cannot break the world.** The heavens are incapable of it, and she is the proof.

The lesson is: **you will be cast down, and then you will serve anyway.**

> *This is what disorder achieves. It does not overturn the order. It achieves only this: that the rebel is cast down, and is set to work, and serves the very thing she defied, in view of everyone, forever.*
>
> *We do not hate the man out of his station. We pity him, as we pity her. He wants what she wanted, and he will get what she got. **The order does not need our anger. It has already won, and it won a very long time ago, and it is still counting out the week above his head while he sleeps.***

**The wish granted as the sentence.** She wanted to run. She runs. That is the horror.

Presumably they deny the fall entirely, or invert it: that she was not cast out but **fled**, or was **wronged**, or that Barjas struck an innocent. Their version of the thunderbolt is a whole document waiting to be written.

## 1. THE PANTHEON

### The Hebdomad [CANON]

Seven gods, seven days. To hold a day IS to be a god. **A god without a day is not a god.** This equivalence is the load-bearing beam of the entire religion and everything else follows from it.

| God | Greek | Day | Origin word | Domains |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Monas | Μονάς | Monda | μόνος / **Monē** | Great Moon, abiding, solitude, asceticism, cosmos |
| Theureus | Θηρεύς | Tuesda | θεός | Authority, sovereignty, caste, just war |
| Firēs | Ϝίρης | Wirsda / Irsda | ἴρις | Wind, breath, sky, fate, travelers |

**Author's originals:** Phira and Sounia were the author's, pre-session. The other five were derived in-session from the author's origin words.

### The two naming laws [CANON]

**I. The god preserves what the day erodes.** Divine names are older than the calendar and were shielded by liturgy; day names wore down in common mouths. Barjas keeps the *B* that Horda lost. Firēs keeps the digamma (Ϝ) that Irsda lost.

**II. Endings carry theology.** **-eus** = sovereign (Theureus alone among the gods; also Menneus). **-ēs** = messenger (Firēs, cf. Hermēs). **-as** = masculine (Barjas, Sophras). **-a / -ia** = the two goddesses (Phira, Sounia), audibly a pair.

> **Who knows this?** The sound-laws are *scholarship*, not doctrine. The Hierophancy states them, but somebody had to derive them. **[NO SOURCE]** for the derivation itself. Candidate: the unnamed philological body. A dissident Hierophant? A Vestrn antiquarian who could see the pattern precisely because he was outside it?

### The pantheon is a treaty [CANON]

Five gods are Hellenic. **Two are not**: Sounia (*sūrya*) and Barjas (*vajra*). They come from a different language and were brought by a different people.

Before the Unification, the Yavanna were **disparate kingdoms** (author's Germany analogy). The Hebdomad is the record of their union: **each founding state surrendered sovereignty and received a day of the week in exchange.** The week is the constitution. You can read it off a calendar.

**The two foreign gods hold the sun and the thunderbolt** (cosmic order + divine judgment). Whoever brought them was not a junior partner. **[OPEN]** Who were they? This is a whole culture waiting to be named.

## 2. THE CENSUS OF HEAVEN [CANON]

**Nineteen heavenly bodies.** This is what grounds the number 19.

**19 bodies → 19 months → 19 days each → 361 = 19².** The year is the census of heaven, squared. Menneus did not invent 361; he took the count of the sky and squared it. (The Hierophancy says he *found* it, and on this they are right, and can prove it by pointing up.)

**Split: 8 wanderers + 11 fixed = 19.**

### The classical spine [CANON]

The seven day-gods **are** the seven classical wanderers (sun + moon + 5 planets) — which is literally where the real-world 7-day week comes from, and both Greek and Indic astronomy built their weeks on exactly these. So "divinity is the calendar" is now literal: to hold a day and to be a wandering light are the same fact seen twice.

### Vertex, the pole [CANON]

**Latin-analog name, and a TRUE COGNATE of Chakravartin.**

- Sanskrit **vartin** ("turner") and Latin **vertex** share the PIE root **\*wert-**, "to turn."
- *vertex* in real Latin meant: a whirl/eddy (water), the crown of the head (the top), and **the pole of the heavens** (the fixed point the sky turns around).

So: **Chakravartin** = the one who *turns* the wheel. **Vertex** = the still point it turns *around*. Same root, opposite motion — cognate the way *convert* and *vertigo* are.

> This implies a **lost common ancestor tongue** behind the Indic-rooted founders and ancient **Sylvan** (the tongue of Sylvargenta). *Vertex* is a Sylvan fossil, carried now in the sundered survivors D'gento and Ojapól; modern speakers may no longer parse it. It is a second, independent proof (alongside the borrowed constellations below) that these peoples touched long ago. **Never glossed in-world.** For the reader and the philologists to find.

### The River Nel [CANON — cross-cultural]

**The Nel is the source of souls**, and **nearly every culture calls it Nel** (the name is cross-cultural). River water becomes a soul, lives in a mortalkin for a time, then **returns to the river** (or to a deity, if the mortalkin worshipped one).

**Its celestial form is also called Nel** (same name up and down). The band of stars *is* the Nel seen from below; the river on earth and the river in the sky are one river in two aspects.

> **The collision with Eutaxia, for free:** the Nel is a river of **flowing, returning** souls — the turning, cyclical cosmology of the old world, written permanently across the sky as one of the 19 bodies. Menneus dammed the Nel *in himself* (the stagnant-river body). **But the Nel overhead keeps flowing, uncensorable, one of the fixed nineteen the census cannot drop.** The evidence against him is a constellation.

### The Court of the Unmoving — the Dii [CANON; details in SCRATCHPAD_sideria.md]

The nine fixed figures are the **Dii** (sing. *Dius*), collectively the **Court of the Unmoving**: the constellations of **Sideria**, the ancient star-faith of Sylvargenta. A cult of the **fixed** sky, the exact inverse of Eutaxia. A schism (the "Zeroth") adds **Vacuus**, the void, as a 0th before the nine.

**What Eutaxia needs from this (the rest lives in `SCRATCHPAD_sideria.md`):**

## 3. LYSIA

### The goddess [CANON]

**Lysia** (Λυσία), from λύσις: *a loosing, a release, an undoing*. Goddess of the small moon. **She has no day of the week.** In a religion where divinity IS the calendar, that is not an oversight; it is her entire theology and the entire theology of the state against her.

**The Empire calls her Mania** (Μανία), *madness*. She is named not as a goddess but as **a diagnosis**. The two words are close enough that an inquisitor and a heretic can dispute the same goddess for an hour and never discover that they are.

### λύσις is political, not personal [CANON]

In an empire assembled from free kingdoms and held together by oath, *to unbind* means:

- **Secession** — loose a people from the union
- **Succession** — loose a crown from a corpse
- **Manumission** — loose a man from his caste

**These are one act.** She is the name of it.

**Secession and succession are the same word.** The Immortal Emperor's project ("there can be no succession") and her domain are the same object seen from opposite sides.

Phira **binds** the oath. Firēs **carries** it. Lysia **unbinds**. The caste order rests on oath and nothing else. **She is not a heresy within Eutaxia. She is its negation.**

### She could not be seated [CANON]

Every other founding god was offered a day and took it. To seat her would have been to write *leaving is permitted* into the constitution of the world. **She could not be bought, because there was nothing the compact could offer that would not have destroyed the compact.**

So she was written out of time instead.

### The Reckoners [PROPOSED, ratified in principle]

Her cult. They keep **the Ledger**: a book of accounts, not a scripture. It records what was taken, from whom, on what night, and what is still owed. **They do not have a gospel. They have a bill.**

**They are necessarily numerate.** Their feast cannot be printed; it must be *computed from the sky*, every generation, by hand. No such thing as a casual Reckoner. A cell that loses its calculator dissolves within the year.

**They are the CONSERVATIVES, not the innovators.** Their feast is the old New Year, the oldest rite in the world. They are not inventing a holy day. **They are recomputing a deleted one.**

### Why they cannot be uprooted [CANON]

**Their rite and their wound are the same act.** They must compute her feast, and her feast is the anniversary of their own murder. Every generation they rebuild the number from the sky, and the number they get is the date they died.

From outside, a people who annually recompute the hour of their own slaughter and then celebrate it look exactly like people who have lost their minds. **Hence *Mania*.**

**The imperial calendar is structurally powerless against them.** An almanac fixes a festival to a date; hers has no date. An inquisitor who wants to find them must first learn to compute the conjunction, which means learning the thing the Severance abolished.

## 4. THE ARITHMETIC (all verified)

### The old world [CANON]

- Week: **8 days**
- Year: **378 days**
- 27 and 14 have LCM = **378**. **The old year WAS the conjunction.** The sisters met once a year, on the same day, forever. New Year was their reunion. The old calendar was *built around her*.

### The Severance [CANON]

- Week: **7 days**
- Year: **361 = 19²**
- **19 months of 19 days**

### Why 361 [CANON]

- **Perfect square.** Closed lattice, 19x19. The year as a grid of stations. Menneus chose it deliberately; he knows the math.
- **Coprime to 378.** The conjunction can never fall twice on the same date in a human lifetime. It drifts **+17 days per cycle**, visits **every day of the year exactly once**, and returns to origin only after **378 years** (361 conjunctions).

> **The two numbers trade places.** 361 conjunctions to complete 378 years. Nobody alive sees the Great Cycle close.

**They took her day. So she takes them all, one at a time.**

### The seam [CANON]

- **361 ÷ 7 = 51 weeks, remainder 4.** Also coprime.
- Menneus **suspended the week**. 51 perfect weeks, then 4 days with no weekday at all, then the week resumes on Monda.
- **The year always begins on Monda** (his patron's day). The square is intact.
- **Four days a year belong to no god.**

### The Bordas conjunction [CANON, computed]

The conjunction falls within the four dayless days **4 times per 378-year Great Cycle**, once on each of the four days, at intervals of **89 years**.

**Four days; four conjunctions; one cycle.** Nobody designed this. It falls out of the numbers.

89 years is longer than a life.

## 6. THE EMPERORS

### Theureus [CANON]

**A man.** The unifier. **First Emperor of Yavanna.** Conquered the kingdoms, seated their gods, died, was raised, took the sovereign **-eus** for it.

1. **[OPEN] Do the observatories serve the throne, or check it?**
   If astronomy legitimizes caste, the Skopoi are an arm of the state. If a realm that drifts out of true has **forfeited its mandate**, they are the only institution in Yavanna capable of deposing an Emperor. Both readings are supported by current doctrine. **The Reckoners would cite the Skopoi's own texts to prove the second.**

2. **[OPEN] Theureus's clergy on the Immortal Emperor.**
   Their god is the standing proof that godhood requires death. **The most obvious fault line inside the imperial church, and it is undefined.**

3. **[OPEN] Who brought Sounia and Barjas?**
   A whole founding culture, unnamed, holding the sun and the thunderbolt.

4. **[OPEN] The small moon's Yavanna name.**

5. **[OPEN] The old faith's name.**

6. **[OPEN] Sixteen months.**

7. **[OPEN] Who derived the sound-laws?** Needs a philological body.

8. **[OPEN] Does anyone in-world know the Bordas/Aphesis convergence?**
   Two conquered peoples reaching independently for the language of debt is the strongest evidence in the world, and **nobody has noticed it.** This is a scene waiting to happen.
