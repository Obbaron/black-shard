## Naming rule [CANON]

- The empire/place is **Yavanna**; the people/language/adjective is **Yona** (the Yona, Yona priests, a Yona month). NOT "Yavan."
- **Vestrn** = the West / the people / the adjective. Two language registers: **High Vestrn** (Dutch analog) and **Low Vestrn** (English analog).

## 2. THE SEVERANCE WAR & THE CONQUEST [CANON]

- **Yavanna invaded across the Great Desert of Wei.** Vestrn was **on the back foot the entire war**, retreating further and further.
- **Good King Olmund** surrendered to save his people.
- **THE SECRET ONLY VESTRN KNOWS:** the Empire won by **luck**. A war in a distant land across a desert is nearly impossible to supply; **had Vestrn kept retreating, the Kingdom would stand today, and the Empire would have starved.** Olmund's mercy is what handed the Emperor a victory he could not otherwise have won.
  - Simultaneously Vestrn's national **humiliation** and its national **consolation.** Written down for 800 years. Appears in NO Yona/Eutaxian document.
- **TERMS OF SURRENDER:**
  - Vestrn is far from the core, so it was allowed to **go on governing itself** as a satellite.
  - BUT: **NO KINGS.** The kingdom was broken into the **Duchies of Vestrn**, and **no Duke may ever hold more than one duchy.** Deliberately fragmented too small to secede or mount an expedition. Freezing Vestrn = the same act as freezing caste and freezing the calendar (the Severance logic).

Candidates: pre-emption (last independent kingdom that could shelter the old faith / Lysian survivors / become a rival); simple imperial momentum; something IN Vestrn (the old "dragon slot," now empty). Leaning pre-emption or banality. UNDECIDED.

## 3. THE OLD FAITH: VANIRISM & LYSARA [CANON, new]

- **VANIRISM:** the old, native Vestrn religion. **Outlawed in the Severance** (which reached beyond Yavanna into every subject land and outlawed local faiths).
- **LYSARA, the WAR-MAIDEN:** head of the Vanir. **Rode before the host and chose who would fall and who would stand** (Freyja-shaped). A thing "grown out of the land itself," older than any Vestrn crown.
  - Her feasts were **the hinges of the old Vestrn year.** Where a Yona month now stands, an old feast of Lysara's often lies buried under it.
  - Now outlawed: her name is said **"low and quick," or not at all.** Altars thrown down.
  - **NO place in the imperial calendar**, and that absence is the point.
- **PARALLEL TO LYSIA [authorial]:** Lysara and Lysia are both cast-out goddesses with no place in the imperial calendar, both alive in the **godless Bordas.** The Empire casts out goddesses wherever it goes. (The Vestrn chronicler knows Lysara well and Lysia only as hearsay; he does NOT connect them, but the reader does.)

### AESIRISM, the imposed replacement [CANON]

- **AESIRISM:** the **Empire-sanctioned** replacement Vestrn faith (the **Aesir**). The collaborationist church.
- Aesir-priests **bless the imperial months**, keep the reeve's holy days, and teach that **Lysara was a demon** and the Vanir were never gods.
- **THE CLEVER CHAIN:** the Empire did not make Vestrn worship Yona gods. It set **Vestrn god against Vestrn god** (Aesir vs Vanir), so oppression **wears a Vestrn face** and the hand that holds them down looks like their own.
- **REAL-WORLD ANALOG:** Norse **Aesir / Vanir**, INVERTED. In myth the Aesir and Vanir warred then merged; here Vanir = outlawed-native (the people's faith / resistance), Aesir = imposed-orthodox (collaborator church).

## 4. THE DRAGONS [CANON, reframed]

- **Vestrn-native creatures.** Real. (The killed thread was ONLY dragon-blood-as-immortality-drug; dragons themselves are canon.)
- **NOW THE SYMBOL of Old Vestrn and the rising.** Not dead, **"driven deep,"** sleeping under the fells; the Empire "set a watch on the deep places and taught its children they were a story." Fire banked, not out. Older than the Empire; "will be here when it is blown sand."
- **Norse register:** worms / wyrms, coiled in the red dark, wing over wing, under the mountains.
- **THE RISING (prophecy, Vestrn reading):** the storm that fells the Empire is **not Barjas's bolt** (the Yona/neutral reading) **but a dragon's waking**, with **Lysara riding before them.** On a godless Bordas, the fells crack, the long sleep ends, the sky burns.
  - Two-perspectives by design: the Yona read the storm-prophecy as their storm-judge Barjas; the Vestrn read it as dragons + Lysara. Both canon; both coexist. See the calendar's *Storm on the Bordas* section.

## 5. THE CALENDAR, FROM THE VESTRN SIDE [CANON, see eutaxian-calendar.md]

Vestrn does not keep its own gods in the imperial calendar; it reads the Yona one under protest and keeps its own names for things.

- **The Bordas** ("borrowed days") = the 4 dayless days (Yona: **Plerosis**; Reckoners: **Aphesis**). Vestrn reads them as a **debt** the year owes and pays late; "a borrowed thing must be given back." Two conquered peoples independently reached for debt-language (Vestrn *borrowed*, Reckoners *remitted*).
- **On the godless Bordas**, the outlawed Lysara "walks", the crack where the old faith survives.
- **The storm-on-a-Bordas prophecy** (the rising) lives here. See §4.
- **Folk-drift of Yona month/day names** into Low Vestrn: Moon-day / Sun-day / Love's-day (transparent matches to Monas/Sounia/Phira); the **Firon muddle** (Wireon + Phiron both heard as "Firon"); Talon/Lark/Furnace folk-etymologies for the star-months (Taleon/Larikon/Phornakon), whose true (Sylvan/Sideria) origin the chronicler does NOT know.
- **`calendar.json`** (the -da/-das months, *Bordas*): **leading theory = it is a Vestrn almanac**, not a Yona one. Its Finno-Ugric moon-names (Iso/Sisar/Kuu) remain unplaced [OPEN].
