## рЯМН What is this?

**The Black Shard** is a paracosm вАФ an original universe documented from its cosmology down to the argument two priests are having about a single day of the year.

Its stage is the continent of **Weilan**, and its heart is the **west**: the shattered **Duchies of Vestrn**, on the far shore beyond the Great Desert of Wei. This is home ground вАФ a land of dukes and baronies, of dwarven halls and elven wood, of a faith the Empire calls barbarous. The **Yavanna Empire** is not the subject here. It is the weather on the eastern horizon: vast, ancient, deathless, and вАФ for now вАФ content to leave the west mostly alone.

> *There is no "Black Shard" within the world. In-world this is **Weilan**, and its western reach is **Vestrn**. The title belongs to the archive вАФ though there is, as it happens, a place called **The Shard** on Vestrn's southern shore.*

This repository is Weilan's canonical archive. But it is **not** a wiki of settled facts. It is a **collection of testimony** вАФ primary sources that contradict one another, each written from inside some faith, court, or grievance. No single document here is *the truth.* The truth is what survives being read against everything else.

## рЯЫ° The West вАФ the Duchies of Vestrn

Once there was a **Kingdom** of Vestrn. There is not one now.

In the **Severance War** the kingdom was broken and forbidden ever to raise a king again вАФ its crown struck into a scatter of duchies and baronies, no duke permitted more than one. In exchange, the west was left its own: its lords, its rainbow-shouldered goddess, its long memory. The Empire watches from across the sand and, mostly, does not reach. **Mostly.**

## рЯМС The Looming East вАФ Yavanna & the Deathless Emperor

The Empire is the thing the west lives beside and tries not to think about. It need not be understood to be feared вАФ but it rewards understanding.

<details>
<summary><b>вЪЦ Eutaxia & the Seven Who Hold the Days</b></summary>

<br>

The Empire's faith is **Eutaxia** вАФ *good order* вАФ whose gods are the **Hebdomad**, the **Seven who hold the days** of the week. To hold a day *is* to be a god; a god without a day is no god at all.

| Day | God | Domains |
|---|---|---|
| Monda | **Monas** | The Great Moon, solitude, asceticism, the cosmos |
| Tuesda | **Theureus** | Authority, sovereignty, caste, just war |
| Wirsda | **FirƒУs** | Wind, breath, sky, fate, travelers, messages |
| Horda | **Barjas** | Judgment, storms, the thunderbolt, trials |
| Frida | **Phira** | Love, marriage, fertility, union, sacred oaths |
| Sorda | **Sophras** | Wisdom, philosophy, the arcane arts, time, prophecy |
| Sunda | **Sounia** | The sun, fire, illumination, cosmic order |

*Once the Seven were **Eight**. The eighth would not keep her station.* See [`docs/religions/eutaxia.md`](docs/religions/eutaxia.md) вАФ **drafted**.

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>вЩЊ Menneus, the Immortal Emperor</b></summary>

<br>

**Menneus** the **Chakravartin** has reigned for over **eight hundred years** and has never died. He is not worshipped вАФ he holds no day, and so by the faith's own rule he is not a god вАФ but he is **venerated** as the second power in heaven and earth, the hand that made the earth resemble the heavens.

He effectively controls all of Weilan: by direct rule in the east, by soft power and long shadow in the west. His grip is vast. It is also **brittle** вАФ for a reason the whole world half-knows and no one can act upon. *(See [The Knot](#knot).)*

</details>

<details>
<summary><b>рЯУњ The Reckoners</b> вАФ the heresy of the abacus</summary>

<br>

A hunted cult who keep a forbidden book, the **Ledger**, and compute a feast that no almanac can hold вАФ because the Emperor deliberately built a calendar in which it *cannot* be scheduled. They cannot be found by watching the calendar; an inquisitor must first learn to compute the very thing the Empire abolished. Numerate to a soul: *a cell that loses its abacus loses its faith within the year.*

</details>

<a id="knot"></a>

## рЯ™Ґ The Knot

Everything above ties into a single thread вАФ the thing that makes the west's quiet resentment more than idle:

> **Menneus does not merely keep time. He is kept *by* it.**
>
> In the Severance he struck the **eighth day** from the calendar вАФ and bound his own deathlessness to the calendar he remade. His life and the year are one document. **Undo the Severance, and the Emperor falls** вАФ and Yavanna's grip on its satellites loosens with him.
>
> It cannot be done by winning a war. To defeat Menneus is to defeat **the one who holds time at bay.**

<details>
<summary><b>рЯМ© The Vestrn Prophecy</b> вАФ the west's long-held hope</summary>

<br>

The Duchies hold that the Empire will fall upon a **Plerosis** вАФ one of the four godless days that close the imperial year вАФ should it coincide with a **great storm**. The Empire's own reckoners have computed that the moons' conjunction *does* fall within those four days four times in every Great Cycle, once on each day, at intervals of eighty-nine years вАФ and they dismiss the prophecy as an oracle that arrives on a knowable date and then demands an unknowable sign.

The west keeps waiting. Storms, after all, belong to a god of **judgment**. Planned: [`docs/religions/eutaxia.md#the-vestrn-prophecy`](docs/religions/eutaxia.md).

</details>

## рЯЧУ The Calendar

<details>
<summary><b>The Eutaxian year</b> вАФ 361 days, seven gods, two moons</summary>

<br>

The imperial year runs **361 days**, a **seven-day week** named for the Seven, tracked against **two moons вАФ Iso and Sisar.** Where the week and the year fail to divide, four godless days вАФ the **Plerosis** вАФ close the year outside the week entirely. It is on those four days that the west's prophecy waits.

Full write-up: [`docs/cosmology/eutaxian-calendar.md`](docs/cosmology/eutaxian-calendar.md) вАФ **drafted**. Structured data: [`reference/calendar.json`](reference/calendar.json). *(The doctrinal "nineteen months of nineteen days" and the file's **eighteen** months don't yet agree вАФ a known open question, laid out in the article.)*

</details>

<a id="philosophy"></a>

## вЬ® Core Philosophy

> This is not a story.
>
> It is a **world**, and stories only pass through it.
>
> Every duchy has a grievance. Every faith has a heresy. Every map has a lie on it somewhere.
>
> Every source here has an author, and every author has a reason to shade the truth. The record does not agree with itself вАФ and that is not a flaw. **That is the point.**
>
> The goal is not perfection. The goal is *believability* вАФ the weight of a world too large for any one voice to hold.
